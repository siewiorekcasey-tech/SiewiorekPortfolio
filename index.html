Travel Page • HTML

My Dream Trip to Japan

This page is built as a simple travel guide for my dream trip to Japan. It uses semantic HTML structure with a header, navigation, sections, lists, and a mini glossary.

Highlights include:

  • A header and navigation bar with links to the food section, an “About this trip” page, and the official Japan travel website.
  • A section describing why I want to visit Japan and what excites me most about the culture.
  • A list of must-visit destinations, like Tokyo and Kyoto, with short explanations of what I’d do there.
  • A “Japanese Food” list that calls out dishes like sushi (with rolls such as salmon nigiri and dragon roll), ramen, takoyaki, and okonomiyaki.
  • An ordered list of steps to prepare for the trip: saving money, getting a passport, booking flights and hotels, learning phrases, and packing.
  • A small “travel glossary” explaining terms such as Shinkansen and other travel-related words.
What I practiced: semantic tags, navigation, external links, ordered and unordered lists, and basic page structure.
Lists • Content

Must-Watch Movies & Series

This project is a small but focused HTML page that lists my top three movies or series everyone should watch at least once.

My list includes:

  • The Godfather – a masterpiece of cinema with deep storytelling, strong character development, and themes of power, loyalty, and family.
  • Pulp Fiction – famous for its out-of-order story, memorable characters, sharp dialogue, and the way it mixes humor with violence.
  • Game of Thrones – a series known for its complex characters and constantly evolving storyline that keeps you wanting more.
What I practiced: basic HTML structure, headings, paragraphs, and simple content layout.
Blog • Article Layout

Cooking Blog: My Love for Fried Chicken

This page is a mini cooking blog built around one of my favorite comfort foods: fried chicken. It uses a blog-style layout with a header, navigation, main article, image, quote, and an aside.

Key elements:

  • A header and simple navigation bar to show how a blog’s top area can look.
  • An article section with a title like “The Art of Perfect Fried Chicken” and a date/author line.
  • An image of golden, crispy fried chicken to add visual appeal to the page.
  • Sections that talk about why fried chicken matters to me, how it connects to family and comfort, and how I like to season and cook it.
  • A highlighted <aside> block for a tip or note, and a <blockquote> to call out a quote in the content.
What I practiced: article semantics, combining text with images, and using aside/blockquote for richer content.
CSS • Styling & Layout

Tech Insights – CSS Styling Assignment

This assignment focuses on styling a tech-themed page. The layout uses a central content column with images, cards, and a comparison table to make the information easier to scan.

Key features:

  • A set of “article cards” with images and text that highlight different tech topics, such as smartphone trends.
  • A responsive grid for the cards so they rearrange nicely on smaller screens.
  • A comparison table that lines up features and prices, practicing borders, header rows, and a footer row.
  • Simple, readable system fonts and spacing for better typography.
What I practiced: external CSS, card design, responsive grid layout, and table styling.
Multi-Page • Navigation

Tech Insights – Multi-Page Website

This project expands the Tech Insights idea into a small website with multiple pages. It includes a homepage, comparison page, and contact page, plus images and navigation links.

Highlights:

  • A homepage that introduces the “Tech Insights” brand with a logo, hero content, and links to articles.
  • A comparison page that uses images and a table to compare different smartphones and their key specs.
  • A contact page that shows basic contact info and reinforces the site’s structure.
  • A shared navigation menu across the pages so users can move around the site.
What I practiced: connecting multiple pages together, building navigation, and organizing assets (HTML + images) into a site.
